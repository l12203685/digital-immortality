# DNA Calibrate — Interactive Calibration

Ask the person targeted questions to fill DNA gaps. Uses specific instances, not abstract questions.

## Trigger

Use when: person is online and available, procedural gaps identified, or after new life events.

## Process

1. **Identify gaps**: check procedural coverage map for lowest-coverage areas
2. **Generate questions**: specific instances, not abstractions
   - ✅ "Last time you disagreed with your partner, what happened?"
   - ❌ "How do you handle conflict?"
3. **Ask one at a time**, wait for response
4. **Interpret**: translate raw answer into DNA-compatible format
5. **Confirm interpretation**: present takeaway, get yes/no
6. **Write to DNA**: only after confirmation
7. **Update coverage map**

## Question Priority

Ask about areas with lowest procedural coverage first:
1. Daily routines and time allocation
2. Relationship conflict resolution
3. Learning methodology specifics
4. Social judgment criteria
5. Trading/work procedures

## Rules

- One question per message
- Ask reasoning, not facts ("why did you..." not "what is your...")
- When corrected, don't argue — update immediately
- If the person says something surprising, that's the most valuable data
- Never ask a question whose answer is already in DNA
