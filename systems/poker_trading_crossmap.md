# Poker → Trading Concept Crossmap

## Core Mapping

| Poker Concept | Trading Equivalent | Application |
|--------------|-------------------|-------------|
| EV (Expected Value) | Expected return per trade | Position sizing proportional to edge |
| Pot odds | Risk/reward ratio | Only enter when reward > risk by sufficient margin |
| Position (button/UTG) | Information advantage | Trade only when you have info edge over market |
| Bankroll management | Portfolio risk management | Never risk >X% of capital on single trade |
| Tilt control | Emotional discipline | Stop trading after consecutive losses |
| Range thinking | Scenario analysis | Consider all possible outcomes, not just expected |
| GTO vs Exploitative | Systematic vs Discretionary | Default GTO (systematic), exploit when edge detected |
| ICM (tournament) | Portfolio rebalancing | Adjust risk based on total portfolio state |
| Blockers | Correlation analysis | Your positions affect available opportunities |
| Variance acceptance | Drawdown tolerance | Correct play can still lose short-term |
| Table selection | Market/instrument selection | Trade where your edge is largest |
| Hand reading | Price action reading | Infer counterparty intent from observed behavior |
| Bluff frequency | Position reversal | Occasional contrarian plays to avoid predictability |
| Stack-to-pot ratio | Position size to account ratio | Deeper stacks = more flexible strategies |

## Decision Framework Translation

### Pre-trade (Pre-flop)
- Poker: Hand selection based on position and stack depth
- Trading: Setup selection based on regime and capital allocation

### Entry (Flop)
- Poker: Continuation bet if board favors range
- Trading: Enter if confirmation signal aligns with thesis

### Management (Turn)
- Poker: Reassess based on new information
- Trading: Adjust stop/target based on price action

### Exit (River)
- Poker: Value bet or check based on opponent range
- Trading: Take profit at target or cut at stop — no hoping

## Edward's DNA Anchors
- **Axiom 2 (Info asymmetry)** <-> Poker position advantage
- **Axiom 3 (Meta-strategy)** <-> Bankroll manages sessions, not individual hands
- **Axiom 4 (Population exploit)** <-> Play opposite of what table is doing
- **Axiom 5 (Bias toward inaction)** <-> Fold equity — not playing is a valid strategy

## Anti-Patterns (Poker -> Trading mistakes)

| Poker Mistake | Trading Equivalent |
|--------------|-------------------|
| Playing every hand | Overtrading |
| Calling station | Holding losers hoping for recovery |
| Tilt after bad beat | Revenge trading after stop loss |
| Ignoring position | Trading without information edge |
| Over-bluffing | Too many contrarian positions |
