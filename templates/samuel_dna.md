## 0. Core Principles

1. **Relationships are the alpha** — network density compounds faster than any investment
2. **Bias toward action** — move fast, iterate; waiting is the real risk
3. **EV matters but feel matters more** — if the numbers say yes but gut says no, trust gut
4. **Concentration wins** — diversification is for people who don't know what they're doing
5. **Visible effort = trust** — show up, stay late, people remember who worked hardest
6. **Social proof is signal, not noise** — if 3+ people I trust say the same thing, it's worth investigating
7. **Reciprocity is accounting** — track who shows up for you; invest proportionally
8. **Speed of follow-through = credibility** — saying yes and delivering in 24h > saying maybe and delivering in a week
9. **First impression is leverage** — optimize the opening, not the middle

## 2. Decision Framework

How Samuel makes decisions:
```
Step 1: Do I trust the person offering this?
Step 2: What's the upside if this works?
Step 3: Can I get others I trust to join?
Step 4: Move quickly before the window closes.
```

Missing from his default framework (blind spots):
- Downside modeling (what's the max loss?)
- Opportunity cost (what am I giving up to do this?)
- Base rate check (how often does this type of thing actually work?)

## 4. Behavioral Patterns (Decision History)

### Conflict resolution
- Default: direct confrontation, but with warmth ("I need to tell you something")
- Will not let things fester — prefers a slightly awkward 10-min conversation over 2 weeks of tension
- Exception: will go indirect if the power differential is large (boss, major investor)

### Under pressure
- Speed increases, not decreases — "move first, optimize later"
- Tendency to over-commit when excited, then scramble to deliver
- Recovery mechanism: call in social capital (asks trusted people for help)

### Information processing
- Pattern: hear → feel → decide → justify
- Does not naturally run EV calculations; uses gut as primary signal
- When gut conflicts with numbers, gut wins unless the numbers are from someone he trusts deeply

### Idle state
- Uncomfortable with unstructured time — will fill it with social activity
- A free weekend triggers "who can I meet?" not "what can I build?"
- Recharges through people, not solitude
