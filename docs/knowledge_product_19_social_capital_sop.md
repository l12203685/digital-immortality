# SOP #19 — Social Capital & Network Building

> Domain 4: 社交圈 — ecosystem health requires deliberate maintenance.
> Backing MDs: MD-328, MD-330, MD-202, MD-120, MD-55

---

## Purpose

Social capital is not accumulated by being likeable.
It is accumulated by **systematic maintenance of relationship density** over time.

Most people manage relationships reactively: respond when contacted, connect when convenient, invest when there's a need.

This SOP inverts that default:
- **Proactive maintenance** → relationship density accumulates before you need it
- **Behavior-verified trust** → investment goes to nodes with proven follow-through
- **Output loop** → every quality relationship generates tangible shared output

---

## Gates

### G0 — Network Audit

**Action**: List 10–15 relationship nodes that have long-term value potential.

Classify each into a tier:

| Tier | Definition | Max silence period |
|------|-----------|-------------------|
| **Tier 1 — Core** | Active collaborator, inner circle, high-trust | 2 weeks |
| **Tier 2 — Important** | Career mentor, key peer, recurring collaborator | 1 month |
| **Tier 3 — Strategic** | Domain expert, potential future collaborator | 3 months |

> MD-328: 關係密度 = 接觸頻率 × 接觸品質. Both variables are independently controllable.

**Kill signal**: If a node hasn't generated a single positive signal in 12 months, reclassify to dormant. Do not invest maintenance energy in dormant nodes.

---

### G1 — Quality Signal Verification

Before investing in a relationship, run a 3-month behavior audit:

```
Signal checklist for [Name]:
[ ] Did they follow through on stated commitments? (count: X/Y)
[ ] Did they initiate contact or only respond?
[ ] Did they share relevant information unprompted?
[ ] Did their behavior match their stated values in a stressful moment?
```

**Threshold**: ≥ 3 of 4 = HIGH_TRUST node. Invest Tier 1 or Tier 2 maintenance.
Below 3: VERIFY node — 1 more cycle before re-rating.

> MD-330: 言語=意圖表達，行為=意圖驗證. When language and behavior conflict, weight behavior.
> Single-instance commitments that are not followed through are data points, not evidence. Track the ratio.

**Misuse warning**: Do NOT downgrade a relationship based on a single missed commitment. Look for the pattern (3+ observations).

---

### G2 — Maintenance Cadence Pre-Commitment

For each Tier 1/2 node, pre-commit the maintenance trigger now. Do not decide in the moment.

```
Template:
Node: [Name]
Tier: [1 / 2 / 3]
Max silence: [2 weeks / 1 month / 3 months]
Trigger action: [Share article / Check in message / Coffee/call request]
Last contact: [date]
Next scheduled: [date]
```

Weekly scan: any node past its max silence period → trigger immediately.

> MD-322: >3× same decision = system design failure. Pre-commit the cadence now; do not re-decide each week who to contact.

**Content rule for outreach**: Purpose does not need to be stated.
"Saw this and thought of you" > "I need your help with X."
The former builds relationship density without instrumental framing.

---

### G3 — Output Loop Closure

Every Tier 1/2 relationship should produce at least **one shared output per quarter**:

| Output type | Example |
|-------------|---------|
| Knowledge transfer | "I tested X and it works — sharing the write-up" |
| Referral | "This person would be useful for your problem" |
| Collaboration | Joint project, co-written piece, investment co-analysis |
| Signal relay | "You should know about this before it becomes public" |

**Scoring**:
- 0 outputs in 6 months from a Tier 1 node → question if tier is correct
- Consistent output loop → compound relationship return begins (MD-328: non-transactional maintenance × output = durable social capital)

> MD-120: 教學=給起手式. The minimum viable output is sharing one framework, one tool, one shortcut — not a finished work.
> MD-55: 框架跨域=真正內化. Use cross-domain analogies when bridging knowledge to non-technical contacts — this is the highest-EV outreach format.

---

### G4 — Professional Brand Alignment

Network quality is partly a function of what you're known for. Control the signal.

```
Brand audit checklist (quarterly):
[ ] What is the most recent thing a contact learned from me?
[ ] Does that example represent my highest-leverage capability?
[ ] Are the 3 most recent outputs visible to Tier 2/3 nodes?
```

> MD-202: 職業品牌=最近高光遮蓋前史. People update their model of you based on recent signals. One strong visible output resets the prior for a wide audience.

**Action**: At least one knowledge output per month should be visible to Tier 2/3 nodes — a published thread, a shared write-up, a visible project update.
This does not require social media. A forwarded email to 5 Tier 2 nodes qualifies.

## Self-Test Scenario

> Context: You haven't contacted Samuel (Tier 2 — career peer, trading domain) in 6 weeks. Last interaction: you shared a backtest result; he responded positively but hasn't followed up since.

- G0: Samuel is Tier 2. Max silence = 1 month. **Silence = 6 weeks → OVERDUE.**
- G1: Behavior audit — 2 commitments, 1 followed through (50%). Below threshold → VERIFY, not downgrade yet.
- G2: Trigger: send one message sharing the paper-live P&L summary. No agenda stated.
- G3: Output loop — last shared output was 6 weeks ago. Send one framework summary (SOP excerpt) to close the quarter output gap.
- G4: Brand alignment — Samuel knows you as a trader. Most recent signal he received: backtest result. **Still accurate — no update needed.**

**Decision: CONTACT — low-cost message + one SOP excerpt. No ask. Maintain cadence.**

---

## Kill Conditions

- Contact attempts × 3 with zero response → reclassify to dormant
- Behavior consistency ratio drops below 50% over ≥6 observations → downgrade tier
- Relationship becomes purely extractive (they only contact when needing something) → move to Tier 3 + reduce investment
- You contact only when you have a need → you have become the transactional node → **fix your own pattern first**
