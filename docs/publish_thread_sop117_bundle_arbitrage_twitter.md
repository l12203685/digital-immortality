# SOP #117 — Twitter Thread Draft

**Posting queue**: ~Dec 2026 (after SOP #116 clears)

---

**Tweet 1/7**
Most arbitrage is competed away instantly.

Bundle arbitrage isn't.

It exists because of an information gap — not a price gap.

Here's the 6-gate protocol for finding and executing it:

SOP #117: Bundle Arbitrage Protocol 🧵

---

**Tweet 2/7**
The structure:

A scarce component is embedded in a bundle.

The bundle is priced by its average or weakest piece.
The scarce component, sold alone, costs significantly more.

The arb window = the time between when you know which piece is scarce and when everyone else figures it out.

---

**Tweet 3/7**
G0 — Scarcity identification

Before anything else: confirm standalone demand exists.

Not "this seems rare."
Not "I couldn't find it easily."

Actual buyers paying actual prices for this specific component, in an accessible market.

No confirmed standalone demand = no arbitrage. Full stop.

---

**Tweet 4/7**
G2 — Spread calculation (before you buy anything)

Net Spread = Standalone Price − Bundle Cost − Transaction Costs − Time Cost

Run three scenarios:
• Base case
• Bear case (buyer haggles -20%)
• Friction blowout (+50% costs)

If net spread goes negative in the bear case: skip.

---

**Tweet 5/7**
G3 — Exit route verification

This is the gate most people skip.

Before acquiring the bundle, answer:
• Who specifically buys the scarce component?
• Where do they transact?
• What's the exit timeline?

No confirmed exit channel = you're not an arbitrageur.
You're a speculator with inventory.

---

**Tweet 6/7**
G4 — Information edge duration

Arbitrage collapses when the information gap closes.

High-transparency market: hours to days → execute immediately or pass
Niche / private market: weeks to months → you have room to be methodical

Rule: if edge duration < acquisition time, do not proceed.

---

**Tweet 7/7**
The two anti-patterns that kill this:

1. Buying without a confirmed exit (G3 skip) — you now own a position, not an arb
2. Chasing widely-known spreads — if it's obvious, it's already competed away

Bundle arbitrage only works while information is asymmetric.
The moment it's symmetric: it's over.

SOP #01–#117 complete. ✅
