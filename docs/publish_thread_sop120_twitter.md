# Twitter Thread — SOP #120: Root Variable Confirmation Protocol

**Posting queue**: Jan 2027  
**Hook**: One yes/no question before your conclusion. That's the whole protocol.

---

**Tweet 1 (hook)**
Before you state your conclusion in any complex negotiation:

Stop.
Ask one yes/no question.

That's SOP #120.

Here's why this saves you from building conclusions on wrong premises 🧵

---

**Tweet 2 (the problem)**
Multi-day negotiations accumulate detail.

By the time you're ready to conclude, you and the other party may think you've been talking about the same thing.

Often you haven't.

The gap lives in one unconfirmed factual assumption.

---

**Tweet 3 (the behavioral trace)**
2017. Complex family negotiation. Multiple days. High stakes.

Before concluding "not entering the clinic," Edward asked:
"Just to confirm — the clinic closes when X leaves, correct?"

One question. One minute. Confirmed the entire premise.

If the answer had been "no" — different conclusion.

---

**Tweet 4 (G1 — find the root variable)**
G1: Identify the root decision variable.

"What single fact, if wrong, changes my entire conclusion?"

Must be factual (not a preference, not a judgment):
✅ "Whether the clinic closes when X leaves"
❌ "Whether this is a good opportunity"

---

**Tweet 5 (G2 — compress)**
G2: Compress it to ONE yes/no question.

Format: "Just to confirm — [factual claim], correct?"

Rules:
— One question only
— No compound "and/or"
— Must be answerable yes/no

If you have two questions, you haven't done G1 right.

---

**Tweet 6 (G3 — ask explicitly)**
G3: Ask out loud. Wait for the answer.

Don't assume you know. Don't fill silence with "I'll take that as yes."

If the answer matches expectation → proceed.
If it diverges → stop, recalibrate before concluding.

---

**Tweet 7 (G4 — anchor the conclusion)**
G4: State your conclusion with the confirmed fact explicit.

"Based on [confirmed root variable], my conclusion is X."

This makes your reasoning auditable.
If the root variable later turns out wrong, the error is traceable.

---

**Tweet 8 (anti-patterns)**
Common failures:
× "I already know this" → Complex discussions have implied facts never stated
× "Asking makes me look inattentive" → Precision confirmation is the opposite
× "I'll confirm after I conclude" → Too late. Re-work cost is higher.

The difference between inference and confirmation: one question.

---

**Tweet 9 (close)**
SOP #120: Root Variable Confirmation Protocol

G0 Detect conclusion proximity
G1 Find root decision variable
G2 Compress to one yes/no
G3 Confirm explicitly
G4 Anchor conclusion to confirmed fact

One question prevents building on wrong premises.

SOP #01~#120 COMPLETE ✅
