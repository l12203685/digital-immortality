# Twitter Thread — SOP #18: Bias Toward Inaction

**Hook tweet:**
> Most traders don't lose because they pick wrong.
> They lose because they trade when they shouldn't.
> 
> Here's the 5-gate protocol I use to decide when to stay idle.
> (Spoiler: default answer is always IDLE)

---

**Tweet 2:**
> The default state isn't "look for trades."
> The default state is IDLE.
> 
> You must earn the right to trade.
> Not the other way around.

---

**Tweet 3 — G0:**
> Gate 0: Name the edge.
> 
> Can you say, in ONE sentence, the structural reason this trade has positive EV?
> 
> "I think it'll go up" = not an edge
> "CT 80% bullish + extreme funding + OI ATH = crowd overextended" = edge
> 
> Can't name it? Stay idle.

---

**Tweet 4 — G1:**
> Gate 1: Asymmetry check.
> 
> reward/risk ≥ 2.0 → proceed
> reward/risk 1.0–2.0 → 50% size only
> reward/risk < 1.0 → idle, no exceptions
> 
> High conviction doesn't override math.

---

**Tweet 5 — G2:**
> Gate 2: Model friction before entry.
> 
> Spread. Slippage. Funding rate. Taxes.
> 
> If net EV after friction < 1.5× risk → idle.
> 
> "I'll figure it out after" is not a plan.
> It's how you lose money efficiently.

---

**Tweet 6 — G3:**
> Gate 3: Regime alignment.
> 
> Trend strategy in mean-reverting market = misaligned → idle.
> Mean-reversion strategy in trending market = misaligned → idle.
> 
> The market doesn't care about your strategy.
> Your strategy has to care about the market.

---

**Tweet 7 — G4:**
> Gate 4: Real opportunity cost check.
> 
> "What do I lose by NOT trading?"
> 
> "Nothing concrete" → idle is correct.
> "Named, time-limited structural edge expires" → execute.
> "Fear of missing out / boredom" → idle. These aren't costs.

---

**Tweet 8 — Self-test:**
> Scenario: BTC ranging sideways. You feel like trading. Signal = FLAT.
> 
> G0: Name the edge → "I'm bored and it might break out"
> 
> That's not an edge. That's FOMO with extra steps.
> 
> Result: IDLE.

---

**Tweet 9 — Close:**
> The protocol isn't about finding reasons to NOT trade.
> It's about forcing explicit justification for action.
> 
> Most of the time, you can't justify it.
> That's the point.
> 
> Preserved capital = preserved optionality.
> Idle isn't dead. Idle is ready.
