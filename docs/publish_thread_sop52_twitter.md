# Twitter Thread — SOP #52: Sleep & Physical Recovery Protocol
> Slot: Jul 20, 2026 | Domain: 8 (生活維護)
> Hook: "You feel fine. Your output says otherwise."

---

**Tweet 1 (Hook)**
You feel fine.

Your work quality has been declining for 5 days.

These two facts are both true. That's why sleep debt is dangerous.

Sleep & Recovery Protocol — 5-gate framework:

---

**Tweet 2 (The Problem)**
Sleep is the first thing traded away under deadline pressure.

And the last thing restored when capacity returns.

Result: chronic partial deprivation running as normal. Every decision, article, and strategy quietly degraded.

---

**Tweet 3 (Core Insight)**
Don't ask: "How tired do I feel?"

Ask: "What is my cognitive performance trajectory over the past 5 days?"

Feeling is corrupted by adaptation. Trajectory tells the truth.

---

**Tweet 4 (G0 — Daily Audit)**
G0: Recovery Audit (5 min, every morning)

Two signals:
→ Hours in bed (Green: ≥7.5h, Red: <6h)
→ Morning cognitive clarity (Green: abstract reasoning within 20 min)

Two consecutive Red mornings = trigger G5 emergency.

---

**Tweet 5 (G1 — Trajectory)**
G1: Performance Trajectory Scan (2 min)

Not "am I tired?" — Compare yesterday's first-pass quality to your 7-day average.

Did complex reasoning take longer? Did reactive responses increase?

Two days of trajectory decline without external cause = sleep is the suspect.

---

**Tweet 6 (G2 — Allocation)**
G2: Recovery Budget (non-negotiable)

→ Core Sleep: 7–8h fixed window
→ Physical Reset: 20–30 min movement or deliberate rest

If a deadline conflicts with the sleep window — the deadline is wrong, not the sleep window. Reschedule work, not recovery.

---

**Tweet 7 (G3 — Context Switching)**
G3: The 60-minute buffer rule

Sleep quality degrades when:
→ Cognitive arousal within 60 min of sleep (open loops, stimulating content)
→ Sleep timing varies ±90 min across the week

Fix: Capture open loops to session_state before sleep window. Accept ±30 min, flag ±60 min, intervene at ±90 min.

---

**Tweet 8 (G4 — Weekly Review)**
G4: Weekly Recovery Review (15 min, Sunday)

Count Red/Yellow nights. If ≥3 Yellow or ≥1 Red: identify cause.
→ Structural: fix schedule
→ Behavioral: fix protocol
→ External: accept, plan recovery week

Target: ≥6 Green nights per week.

---

**Tweet 9 (G5 — Emergency)**
G5: Recovery Emergency

Triggered when ≥3 Red nights in 7 days.

1. Sleep window becomes the highest-derivative activity
2. Add 1h to window for 5 days (debt isn't linear — can't catch up in one night)
3. No major decisions, no system edits until 3 consecutive Green nights

---

**Tweet 10 (Three-Layer Loop)**
Recovery maps onto the three-layer loop:

→ L1 Execute: sleep = uptime for the system running the daemon
→ L2 Evaluate: sleep debt produces false negatives — missed drift, accepted lower quality
→ L3 Evolve: no DNA edits during recovery emergency (requires full cognitive presence)

L2 failure under sleep debt is invisible until the system has already drifted.

---

**Tweet 11 (Anti-Pattern)**
Most common failure:

"I'll sleep when the deadline is done."

The deadline requires the cognition the deprivation is destroying.

You're not trading sleep for productivity. You're trading future clarity for present motion.

---

**Tweet 12 (Close)**
Recovery isn't laziness.

It's maintenance of the execution layer that runs every other system.

A system running on degraded hardware is the waste. Recovery is the fix.
