# Twitter Thread — SOP #16: Information Edge Classification

> Hook: "You don't have an edge. You have a belief. These are not the same thing."
> Format: 9 tweets. One claim per tweet. Last tweet = CTA.

---

**Tweet 1 (Hook)**
You don't have an edge.
You have a belief.

These are not the same thing.

Most traders never classify what type of advantage they actually hold before entering a position.

Here's a 4-gate SOP to find out if your edge is real. 🧵

---

**Tweet 2 (The 3 edge types)**
Edge has 3 types. You need to name one before entry:

→ Structural: access others don't have (data, speed, venue)
→ Temporal: you act earlier in a known sequence
→ Interpretive: you process the same data at a higher thinking level

If you can't name one: that's a belief, not an edge. Abort.

---

**Tweet 3 (G0 — Classify)**
Gate 0: Classify your edge type before every trade.

Not as a ritual. As a filter.

The moment you try to name the edge type and can't — you've already discovered the position is built on narrative, not structure.

That's worth the 10 seconds.

---

**Tweet 4 (G1 — Public signal decay)**
Gate 1: Is your signal public?

Once a setup is in a strategy guide, a Twitter thread, or a YouTube video — adoption spreads. Edge decays.

Formula:
edge_remaining = original_edge × (1 − adoption_fraction)

Adoption ≥ 50%? You need G2 confirmation or step back.

---

**Tweet 5 (G2 — Thinking level)**
Gate 2: What level are you operating at vs. the market?

L1 = reacting to price
L2 = modeling what others think about price
L3 = modeling what others think others think

You need L+1 over your competition. If everyone's at L2 and you're at L2, you are average.
Average = no edge.

---

**Tweet 6 (Thinking level example)**
Example:

Market reacts to CPI headline → L1
You're trading the regime-shift implied by the 6-month CPI trend → L3

Delta = +2. Proceed.

But if your thesis is the same as the CT consensus short setup posted by 3 traders this morning?

Delta = 0. That's belief.

---

**Tweet 7 (G3 — Hidden filters)**
Gate 3: What's your implicit filter?

If you've ever skipped your own system's signal — you have a hidden rule you haven't named.

Options:
→ Name it → add it to the system → apply it consistently
→ Can't name it → it's noise → execute the system signal

Overrides without named rules = system corruption.

---

**Tweet 8 (Self-test)**
Self-test:
BTC SHORT signal from your system. CT is also bearish. Same setup posted 3 times today.

G0: Interpretive edge (regime classification) ✓
G1: Public adoption HIGH → edge decayed → reduce size to 50%
G2: If crowd = L1, you = L2 → delta +1 → proceed
G3: "Feels risky" = unnamed filter → suppress override → execute

Result: CONDITIONAL GO, 50% size.

---

**Tweet 9 (CTA)**
The goal isn't to find a reason to avoid the trade.
It's to know exactly what you're betting on — and whether anyone else already knows it.

Classify your edge. Verify it's still alive. Name every hidden rule.

Everything else is narrative.
