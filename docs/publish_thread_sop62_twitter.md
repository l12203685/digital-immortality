# Twitter Thread — SOP #62: Social Capital & Relationship Investment Protocol

> Slot: Aug 7, 2026 | Domain: 4 (Social Capital)
> Hook: "You don't have a relationship problem. You have an investment model problem."

---

**Tweet 1 (Hook)**
You don't have a relationship problem.

You have an investment model problem.

Most people manage relationships reactively. The ones who compound manage them like assets.

Here's the SOP I use: 🧵

---

**Tweet 2**
The core insight:

Social capital compounds — or decays.

Every relationship is either:
→ Growing (proactive cadence, collision, output)
→ Decaying (reactive, no follow-through, no closure)

There is no neutral. Neglect is a choice.

---

**Tweet 3**
G0 — Relationship Inventory Audit

Map your graph every month:
- T1: Close collaborators (calibration partners, high-trust loops)
- T2: Periodic reinforcement
- T3: Weak ties with option value

Kill condition: any T1 relationship >30 days uncontacted → emergency mode.

---

**Tweet 4**
G1 — Derivative Scan

Don't measure relationship level. Measure the rate of change:

→ Is T1 contact frequency rising or falling?
→ Is collaborative output increasing?
→ Are you scheduling organism collisions or canceling them?

All three falling = attention budget leak. Find and redirect.

---

**Tweet 5**
G2 — Non-Negotiable Investment Budget

These are not suggestions:
- ≥1 proactive message per T1 per 14 days (scheduled, not reactive)
- ≥1 calibration session per month
- Close every loop within 7 days of an intro or referral
- No ghosting after high-context conversation (48h follow-up)

Budget your attention before it gets stolen.

---

**Tweet 6**
The proactive cadence principle (MD-328):

Relationship investment is a scheduled output — not a reactive response.

"I'll reach out when I have something to say" = decay.

"I reach out on cadence, regardless" = compounding.

The difference is structural, not motivational.

---

**Tweet 7**
G3 — Quarterly Organism Leverage Scan

Once a quarter, ask:
- Which relationship has highest compounding potential (EV × time horizon)?
- Which person would surface the most decision divergence in a collision?
- Who's the next organism to model?

Pick one. Run a 90-day trial. Measure the output.

---

**Tweet 8**
"Organism collision" — the underrated tool:

Two people making decisions from different models surface what solo reflection cannot.

When your calibration partner disagrees: that's not friction. That's signal.

Divergence reveals edge cases in your value structure.

---

**Tweet 9**
G4 — Weekly Review (10 min Sunday)

Answer:
1. T1 contacts made this week vs scheduled?
2. Organism calibration: on track this month?
3. Any pending follow-ups >48h? (Resolve now)
4. Next highest-derivative relationship move?

10 minutes prevents months of drift.

---

**Tweet 10**
G5 — Social Debt Protocol

Trigger: T1 >30 days, OR calibration gap >45 days, OR ≥3 follow-ups outstanding.

Recovery:
1. Send one proactive message to highest-priority T1 today (short is fine)
2. Schedule calibration within 7 days
3. Clear all outstanding follow-ups first

Social debt compounds faster than financial debt.

---

**Tweet 11**
The three-layer loop:

L1 Execute → G2 investment budget (non-negotiable cadence)
L2 Evaluate → G0/G1 audit (detect decay early)
L3 Evolve → G3 leverage scan (highest-EV relationship this quarter)

Execute without Evaluate = drift.
Evaluate without Evolve = stagnation.
All three = compound.

---

**Tweet 12 (Close)**
Social capital is the one asset that:

→ Grows faster when you give it away
→ Decays fastest when you're reactive
→ Compounds non-linearly with calibration

Most people wait for relationships to happen.
The ones who compound schedule them.
