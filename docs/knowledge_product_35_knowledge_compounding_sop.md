# SOP #35 — Knowledge Compounding & Skill Half-Life Protocol

> Stop learning that evaporates. Build learning that compounds.
> DNA anchors: MD-329/163/319/55/321/46

---

## The Problem

Most learning systems optimize for input volume — books read, courses finished, notes taken.
None of that matters if the knowledge cannot be retrieved, applied, or transferred to a new domain.
This SOP enforces the six structural conditions that separate compounding knowledge from consumable information.

---

## Gate 0 — Skeleton Constraint

**Trigger:** Beginning any new subject, domain, or body of work.

**Rule (MD-329):** Build 20% framework BEFORE absorbing any detail.
Skeleton first: table of contents, landscape survey, core concepts map.
No exceptions. Entering detail before skeleton = knowledge fragments that cannot be retrieved.

**Action:**
1. Write the TOC of what you expect to learn — before reading.
2. Identify the 3–5 load-bearing concepts that everything else hangs from.
3. Map known analogies: what does this look like in a domain you already own?

**Pass criteria:** You can draw a one-page concept map with 5+ nodes before opening the first source.

**Kill condition:** If you are 3+ hours into a subject and cannot draw its skeleton → stop. Build the map. Then return.

---

## Gate 1 — Input Quality Audit

**Trigger:** Selecting any source (book, course, person, article, dataset).

**Rule (MD-163):** Input quality sets the ceiling. Effort cannot compensate for a low-quality source.
A practitioner's 5-page memo outperforms a theorist's 400-page book.

**Audit checklist — rate each source on:**
- Practitioner vs. theorist: does this person have skin-in-the-game? (Lost money? Staked reputation?)
- Primary vs. derivative: is this the original claim, or a summary-of-a-summary?
- Incentive alignment: what does this author gain by being right or wrong?
- Recency vs. half-life: is this a decaying technical skill or a permanent framework?

**Pass criteria:** Source scores ≥2/4 on the checklist. If it fails, deprioritize — no amount of time fixes a low-ceiling source.

**Kill condition:** If you cannot identify who the original practitioner is → the source is derivative. Trace back or replace.

---

## Gate 2 — Output Forcing Function

**Trigger:** End of every learning session (book chapter, study block, deep read).

**Rule (MD-319):** Output is the gap detector. If you cannot produce output, you did not learn — you consumed.
Teaching reveals exactly which nodes are missing. Explain a concept and watch where you stall.

**Required output — produce ONE of:**
- A note with a concrete decision rule: "In situation X, do Y because Z."
- A tweet or thread draft explaining the concept to a peer.
- A test prediction: "Based on this framework, I predict X will happen in domain Y."
- A question answered: someone asks you to explain this — write the reply.

**Pass criteria:** Output exists. Length is irrelevant. Absence is failure.

**Kill condition:** Learning without output for >7 days → invoke G2 immediately before any further input. You have accumulated without integrating. The queue is stale.

---

## Gate 3 — Cross-Domain Transfer Test

**Trigger:** After reaching preliminary fluency in any framework (not facts — frameworks).

**Rule (MD-55):** True internalization = cross-domain transfer.
If you can only apply a principle in the domain where you learned it, you memorized surface patterns, not the underlying structure.
Frameworks are universal. Facts are local.

**Transfer test protocol:**
1. Name the principle you believe you understand.
2. Apply it to a completely unrelated domain (trading → negotiation, option theory → career decisions, Kelly criterion → time allocation).
3. If the transfer produces a non-obvious insight in the new domain → framework internalized.
4. If the transfer fails or produces only surface analogies → downgrade to "fact" in memory system.

**Pass criteria:** Successful transfer to ≥1 unrelated domain with a non-trivial insight.

**Kill condition:** Three failed transfer attempts → reclassify from "framework" to "fact." Facts are useful but not compounding. They do not generalize. Label them correctly and stop expecting leverage from them.

---

## Gate 4 — Skill Half-Life Audit

**Trigger:** Quarterly review, or when a skill has not been used in 60+ days.

**Rule:** Skills decay at different rates. Misallocating maintenance time is the primary cause of knowledge debt.

**Half-life taxonomy:**
| Skill Type | Approximate Half-Life | Maintenance Requirement |
|---|---|---|
| Specific tools (software, APIs, syntax) | 2–4 years | Review every 6–12 months or on use |
| Domain-specific techniques (options models, ML methods) | 5–7 years | Annual review, active use prevents decay |
| Strategic frameworks (game theory, systems thinking) | 10–15 years | Refresh via application, not study |
| Mental models (EV thinking, base rates, inversion) | Permanent | No decay if applied regularly |

**Audit action:**
1. List active skills by category.
2. Identify skills not used in 90+ days.
3. For each: schedule review, archive, or accept decay and do not rely on it.

**Pass criteria:** Every skill in active use has a maintenance cadence assigned. Decay zone skills are either scheduled or archived — not assumed to be current.

**Kill condition:** Relying on a skill that has not been exercised in 90+ days without verification → stop. Verify current state before depending on it in any decision.

---

## Gate 5 — Knowledge Product Creation Gate

**Trigger:** After accumulating enough on a topic to potentially teach it.

**Rule (MD-321):** Productizing forces all implicit steps to surface.
A body of knowledge is not internalized until it can be written as a teachable SOP.
If you cannot write the SOP, you have gaps. The SOP-writing process is the gap-finding process.

**Action:**
1. Attempt to write a 5-gate SOP for the subject.
2. Wherever you stall → that is the gap. Fill it, then continue.
3. If the SOP can be completed → the knowledge is internalized enough to teach, which means it is internalized enough to apply reliably.

**Pass criteria:** A complete, teachable SOP exists. Can be given to someone with no prior context and produce correct action.

**Kill condition:** If you cannot write even a 3-gate SOP after substantial study → learning has not been structural. Return to G0 and rebuild the skeleton.

## Self-Test Scenario

**Setup:** You have spent 3 weeks reading option theory books.
You can explain Black-Scholes. You cannot say where you would enter a trade.

Apply the gates:

**G0 — Did you build a skeleton first?**
If not: draw the concept map now. Core concepts: underlying price, volatility, time decay, strike selection, risk/reward profile.
If you cannot fill in this map, the 3 weeks produced fragments, not structure. Rebuild.

**G1 — What was the input quality?**
Were you reading practitioners (traders who lost money on bad options positions) or theorists (academics who derived the formula)?
If primarily theorists: the ceiling is mathematical elegance, not trading edge. Source quality capped your outcome before you started.

**G2 — Can you produce one output right now?**
Write one decision rule: "I will consider buying a call when [specific conditions] and will not pay more than [specific premium threshold] because [specific reason]."
If you cannot complete that sentence → you consumed, you did not learn. Write it now before reading another page.

**G3 — Can you apply option thinking to salary negotiation?**
Salary negotiation contains: optionality (keep applying = stay long on alternatives), time value (offer expires, urgency asymmetry), exercise decision (take now vs. wait for better strike), volatility (how uncertain is the hiring market?).
If this transfer produces zero non-trivial insight → you have Black-Scholes as a fact. You do not have optionality thinking as a framework.

**G5 — Could you write a 6-gate SOP for option selection?**
G0: Build the payoff diagram skeleton. G1: Audit the volatility surface source. G2: Produce a trade prediction before entering. G3: Apply the position sizing rule from Kelly. G4: Identify the half-life of the vol regime. G5: Document the trade as a teachable case.
If you cannot draft those gates → continue until you can. Do not claim the knowledge until the SOP is possible.

## Kill Conditions Summary

| Condition | Threshold | Response |
|---|---|---|
| Learning without output | >7 days | Invoke G2 immediately. No further input until output exists. |
| Failed cross-domain transfer | 3 attempts | Downgrade from "framework" to "fact" in memory system. Stop expecting leverage. |
| Skill in decay zone, unused | >90 days | Schedule review or archive. Do not rely on it in decisions without verification. |
| Cannot draw skeleton | >3 hours into subject | Stop. Build the map. Return. |
| Cannot write 3-gate SOP | After substantial study | Return to G0. The learning was not structural. |
| Low-quality source ceiling | Fails G1 audit | Replace source. Effort cannot compensate. |

---

## The Compounding Equation

MD-46: Knowledge = time × density.

Density is determined by: input quality (G1) × structural skeleton (G0) × output forcing (G2) × transfer depth (G3).

Time spent on low-density inputs compounds toward zero.
Time spent on high-density inputs with structural absorption compounds permanently.

The choice is not "more learning" vs. "less learning."
The choice is: which inputs are above the compounding threshold, and are you applying the gates that prevent evaporation?
