**Tweet 2 — Problem**
Most creators post when they finish something.

Wrong sequence.

Distribution is not the last step.
It's the closing gate of the knowledge loop.

No distribution = private memo, not a system.

---

**Tweet 3 — G0: Platform fit**
Before writing anything: score your platform.

audience density × feedback speed ≥ threshold

Twitter/X: trading community = HIGH × FAST ✓
LinkedIn: career audience = MED × MED (acceptable)
Random blog: LOW × SLOW → skip

Fail G0 = don't post there.

---

**Tweet 4 — G1: Gap detection**
Every post needs a one-sentence gap statement FIRST.

"What belief does my audience hold that is wrong or incomplete?"

Bad: "Here's my position sizing process"
Good: "95% of traders size positions wrong. The fix takes 5 minutes."

Gap clarity → hooks write themselves.

---

**Tweet 5 — G2: Hook = asymmetry signal**
The hook is not a summary.
It's a signal that you know something they don't.

Reader must feel the cost of not reading.

If your hook could apply to anyone → it will reach no one.
Specificity is the edge.

---

**Tweet 6 — G3: One claim per thread**
One SOP = one thread = one falsifiable claim.

Structure: Hook → Problem → Framework (≤3 gates) → Self-test → CTA

If you can't write it in ≤9 tweets, the claim is too broad.
Compress until it hurts. That's the edge.

---

**Tweet 7 — G4: Feedback loop (48h close)**
Post → collect signal → update or kill.

Signal = reply quality, saves, DMs
NOT: likes, followers, impressions

No replies after 48h → hook failed → rewrite G2
Confused replies → framework too dense → rewrite G3

Every post is a data point.

---

**Tweet 8 — G5: Compounding check (monthly)**
Distribution compounds when each post builds audience that amplifies the next.

Score monthly (≥2 to continue):
□ Follower growth > 0 this week
□ ≥1 inbound DM or collab signal
□ ≥1 save by someone with domain credibility

Score 0/3 for 30 days → pivot platform.

---

**Tweet 9 — CTA + self-test**
