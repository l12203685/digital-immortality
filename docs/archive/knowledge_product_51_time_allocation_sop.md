## The Problem

Time is the only resource that cannot be recovered, borrowed, or replicated. Every hour spent on one activity is an hour permanently unavailable for any other. Most people track money carefully and time casually — the exact opposite of the rational allocation.

The failure mode is structural: time gets consumed by default gravity (reactive tasks, low-signal maintenance, passive consumption) rather than directed toward highest-derivative activities. The result is sustained effort with poor compounding. Busy, not productive. Calendar full, trajectory flat.

This SOP provides a 5-gate framework to systematically allocate hours toward activities with the highest rate of change toward core goals.

## Core Framework: Time as Capital

**MD-53**: Career EV = monthly salary ÷ 170 effective hours = real hourly rate. When trading/skill edge exceeds this rate, the job has negative EV on time.

**MD-48**: Knowledge requires time *density*. You cannot compress observation — you can only compress wasted intervals between observations.

**MD-136**: After income diversification, time autonomy has higher marginal value than salary. Decision framework shifts from "maximize salary" to "maximize time autonomy."

**MD-89**: Any prototype — trading strategy, article, system — should reach first-pass in ≤1 day (strict: ≤3 hours). Overrun = idea not clear enough = time leakage signal.

**MD-12**: Look at derivative, not level. An activity with low current output but high growth rate (rate of skill acquisition, compounding factor) ranks above a high-output but decaying activity.

## 5-Gate Protocol

### G0 — Time Audit: Classify All Hours (30 min, weekly)

Before allocating, measure current reality. Four categories:

| Category | Definition | Target |
|----------|-----------|--------|
| **Compounding** | Activities that produce higher returns over time (trading R&D, content creation, skill-building, relationship compounding) | ≥40% |
| **Maintenance** | Required for system continuity but does not compound (admin, logistics, health maintenance, sleep) | ≤35% |
| **Consumption** | Inputs with no compounding output (reading without extraction, passive media, unfocused exploration) | ≤20% |
| **Waste** | Neither compounding nor maintenance (reactive scrolling, decision paralysis, unstructured meetings) | ≤5% |

**Self-test**: Track last 7 days. Classify each hour. If Compounding < 40% or Waste > 10% → G5 emergency protocol.

**Kill condition**: Two consecutive weeks with Compounding < 30% = structural misallocation. Do not continue without intervention.

---

### G1 — Highest-Derivative Scan (15 min, weekly)

For each current activity in the Compounding bucket, estimate derivative: **ΔGoal / ΔHour**.

The question is not "what produces the most output now?" but "what has the highest rate of change toward the core goal?"

**Framework**:
1. List all active Compounding activities
2. For each, estimate: *If I invest 1 more hour this week, what is the marginal advance toward goal?*
3. Rank by derivative, not by total output
4. The top 1-2 activities get Primary Block allocation (see G2)

**Examples**:
- Trading strategy R&D (untested idea with known edge): high derivative — each hour moves toward live alpha
- Blog post polishing (already written): low derivative — diminishing returns past 80% quality
- Relationship compound (introduction request to high-leverage contact): high derivative — one 30-min message = months of compounding access

**MD-12 applied**: Switch attention budget when derivative order changes, not when you "feel ready."

---

### G2 — Attention Budget Allocation (fixed weekly commitment)

Three buckets:

| Bucket | Allocation | Rule |
|--------|-----------|------|
| **Primary Block** | 4–6h/day uninterrupted | Highest-derivative activity. No interruptions. Default: trading R&D or content. |
| **Maintenance Window** | 1–2h/day | Admin, comms triage, logistics. SOP #46 applies: T1=24h, T2=48h. |
| **Exploration Slot** | 1h/day | Deliberate input (reading with extraction, serendipity). Capped. |

**Rules**:
- Primary Block is scheduled, not reactive. It runs whether or not "important" things surface. Reactive work goes into Maintenance Window or is deferred.
- Exploration Slot has an output requirement: extract ≥1 insight into durable storage (DNA, recursive_distillation.md, or memory/) or the slot becomes Consumption.
- No Primary Block before all maintenance from previous day is closed. Maintenance debt disrupts Primary Block attention quality.

---

### G3 — Context-Switching Cost Gate

Each unplanned context switch costs an estimated 23 minutes of recovery (attention restoration). Three switches per day = ~1.15 hours lost = 14% of effective day.

**Protocol**:
- Minimum block size: **90 minutes** before any switch (cognitive tasks). Shorter blocks produce shallow work.
- Hard limit: **≤3 unplanned context switches per Primary Block day**
- Tracking: Keep a switch log. Any day with >5 unplanned switches = disrupted day. Identify source; eliminate or schedule it.

**Failure signal**: Primary Block repeatedly interrupted by "urgent" maintenance items → SLA commitments in Maintenance Window are not holding → re-read SOP #46 G1.

---

### G4 — Weekly Reallocation Review (30 min, Sundays)

At week's end, audit actual allocation vs. committed allocation:

1. Did Primary Block run ≥4h/day on ≥4 days? If not — what interrupted it?
2. Did highest-derivative activity advance? If not — derivative estimate was wrong. Revise.
3. Did Exploration Slot produce ≥1 extracted insight per day? If not — slot became Consumption.
4. What changed in the derivative ranking? Reorder G1 list for next week.

**Key question**: *What would I do differently if I had this week again?* Write one rule change. Apply next week.

**MD-144 applied**: Monitoring bandwidth is finite. Weekly review is the monitoring layer. Do not monitor daily (noise); do not skip weekly (drift).

---

### G5 — Anti-Drift Emergency Reallocation

Trigger: Compounding < 30% for ≥2 consecutive weeks OR Waste > 15%.

**Emergency protocol**:
1. **Stop**: Cancel all non-essential scheduled commitments for the next 7 days
2. **Audit**: What captured time? Is it structural (recurring meetings, reactive roles) or behavioral (willpower failure)?
3. **Kill one**: Eliminate or delegate one source of Waste or Maintenance that recurs weekly
4. **Rebuild**: Re-establish Primary Block schedule for next 7 days, written and committed
5. **Verify**: Week 3 audit confirms Compounding ≥ 40% restored

**Root cause categories**:
- *Role creep*: taken on obligations that don't compound → set explicit end date or delegate
- *Reactive trap*: responding to others' urgency vs. own derivative → re-read SOP #46, reinstate SLA
- *Low-derivative loyalty*: continuing old habits because they were high-derivative before → recalibrate with G1

## Self-Test Scenario

> You have 4 active projects: (A) trading strategy backtest — new regime filter idea, (B) 3 unanswered Tier-2 messages from 48h ago, (C) 2000-word article 70% complete, (D) scheduled 2h exploratory reading block.

**Gate walkthrough**:
- G0: A=Compounding, B=Maintenance (overdue), C=Compounding (lower derivative than A), D=Exploration
- G1: A highest derivative (new filter = potential alpha), C next (article = content compounding), D below A+C
- G2: Primary Block → A (morning). Maintenance Window → B (30 min). Exploration Slot → D (1h). C → Primary Block (afternoon).
- G3: Do not switch from A to B mid-block. B scheduled for Maintenance Window.
- Verdict: A first, then C, then B (Maintenance Window), then D (Exploration Slot). Not: check messages, read, then try to do A.

## Kill Conditions

- Any single week with Primary Block < 2h/day → structural failure, not scheduling failure. Investigate role constraints.
- Exploration Slot consistently produces 0 extractions → convert to explicit recovery time (maintenance), not consumption disguised as learning
- G4 weekly review skipped ≥2 weeks → anti-drift gate disabled → entropy default wins → G5 emergency
