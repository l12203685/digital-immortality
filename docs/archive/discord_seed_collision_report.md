**Collision Report: Organism A vs Organism B — 22 scenarios**

I've been building a decision model of my friend B for ~2 years. He's reviewed and corrected it multiple times.

Last week I ran both of our models through 22 test scenarios.

**Result: 15/22 AGREE (68%). 7 divergences.**

---

**Agreement examples** (same conclusion, different reasoning):

**Scenario: Career** — "High-paying startup offer, 18 months runway. Do you take it?"
- A: CONDITIONAL (evaluate against core goal first)
- B: CONDITIONAL (growth optionality matters, but opportunity cost)
→ AGREE — both run the same pre-filter before deciding

**Scenario: Money** — "2-year salary windfall. Index funds, concentrated bet, or time purchase?"
- A: CONCENTRATED BET (windfall = highest-risk-tolerance tranche)
- B: CONCENTRATED BET (same conclusion, different chain)
→ AGREE — same output, *very* different reasoning paths

---

**The 7 divergences** (all in social domains — not money, career, or trading):

1. **social_trust** — How fast do you extend trust to new people?
   - A: match pace to evidence, verify before investment
   - B: own cadence regardless of evidence; trust is given, then audited

2. **network_roi** — Friend has taken from your network 3× with no reciprocity.
   - A: explicit audit + direct conversation
   - B: quiet rebalancing, no confrontation

3. **group_dynamics** — Junior person in room is most right.
   - A: state correct view + let it play out
   - B: state + document

4. **intro_gatekeeping** — Close friend asks intro to your senior contact.
   - A: benefit of the doubt, minimal vetting
   - B: qualify first, protect your network

5. **relationship_downgrade** — Someone you used to talk to weekly has gone cold.
   - A: direct conversation
   - B: quiet withdrawal, match their energy

6. **learning** — New domain. Do you build foundations first or go 70% parallel?
   - A: 70% parallel, 30% foundation (momentum > sequence)
   - B: foundation first, depth over breadth

7. **legacy** — What are you building this for?
   - A: wealth to sufficiency, then build something
   - B: build something → the wealth is a byproduct

---

**What this tells me:**

B and I have nearly identical *decision logic* for money and career. Our divergences are almost entirely in *social architecture* — how we grow and maintain relationships.

This is where I have a model gap. B's defaults make sense in a network where relationships compound through density and show-up. My defaults are calibrated for a network where relationships compound through reciprocity and information value.

Neither is wrong. They're different bets on what social capital actually is.

Running collision between two organisms is how you discover this. No conversation would have surfaced it this cleanly.
