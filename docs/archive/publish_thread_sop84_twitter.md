# Twitter Thread — SOP #84: Engagement-to-DM Conversion Funnel
> Cycle 249 | Domain: Distribution / Revenue

---

Tweet 1 (Hook):
You posted the thread. 47 likes. 8 replies.

Zero DMs.

Not because people aren't interested. Because no one showed them the door.

🧵 Engagement-to-DM Conversion — the missing layer between posting and revenue.

---

Tweet 2:
The failure mode isn't bad content.

It's content that performs — replies, questions, challenges — and then dies because no one responds in time.

After 4 hours: conversion probability drops 60%.
After 24 hours: the conversation is over.

The reply window is real. Most people miss it.

---

Tweet 3:
Classify every reply into 4 buckets:

Q — Question (highest conversion)
C — Challenge (high conversion)
A — Agreement (social proof, low solo conversion)
N — Noise (ignore)

Q and C replies are your pipeline. Everything else is vanity metrics.

---

Tweet 4:
Q reply response formula:

1. Answer in 1-2 sentences (competence, not comprehensiveness)
2. End with: "DM me if you want to go deeper on this."

Never 3+ sentences. Long replies are ego. Short replies with a DM invite are conversion.

---

Tweet 5:
The proactive DM gate (most people skip this):

If the same person replies 3 times in one thread → don't wait.

Reply publicly: "Going to DM you — I have a few questions about your situation."

Then DM immediately.

Three exchanges = demonstrated intent. Passive waiting = zero pipeline.

---

Tweet 6:
Profile audit: run weekly.

Bio: does it answer "what do I get if I follow you"?
Pinned: is it your highest-performing post?
Follow-back rate: are engaged accounts following?

If follow-back rate < 0.5% of impressions → your bio is breaking the funnel.

Fix the bio before posting more threads.

---

Tweet 7 (CTA):
The full system: post (SOP #83) → classify replies → respond within 4h → proactive DM invite at 3 exchanges → track rate weekly → update milestone tracker.

Each step is a gate. Miss one and the pipeline stalls.

Follow for next one. DM me if you want to go deeper.
