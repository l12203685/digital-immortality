> Slot: Oct 21, 2026 (every 2 days from #97 Oct 17)
> Hook: "Your AI passed all the machine tests. Now find the humans."
> Domain: Digital immortality / behavioral AI

---

**Tweet 1 (hook)**

Your AI passed all the machine tests.

33/33 deterministic alignment.
100% cross-instance agreement.
LLM scenarios: validated.

There's one test left.

The only test that actually matters.

🧵 SOP #98: Turing Test Candidate Selection

---

**Tweet 2 (the gap)**

Cross-instance consistency proves internal coherence.

It doesn't prove external equivalence.

The ceiling of behavioral AI validation is:

> A person who knows you well, in a blind evaluation, cannot reliably distinguish the AI from you.

No machine test closes this gap. Only a human can.

---

**Tweet 3 (the blocker)**

Most people building digital twins never get to this stage.

Not because the protocol is hard.

Because they can't answer one question:

*Who do you ask?*

Wrong evaluator → meaningless result.
No evaluators → protocol stays theoretical forever.

---

**Tweet 4 (the criteria)**

A qualified evaluator must meet ALL of:

• Knows you ≥ 3 years
• Observed you in ≥ 2 domains (work, money, relationships, trading)
• Has had ≥ 10 substantive conversations with you
• Can commit 2 sessions × 45 min in 2 weeks
• Agrees to genuine blind evaluation

Family members: disqualified (emotional contamination).
Single-domain colleagues: disqualified (domain coverage).

---

**Tweet 5 (the shortlist method)**

Run this scan against your actual network:

Tier A: Pre-2023 friends with maintained contact
Tier B: Former close colleagues + people who've given you direct feedback on decisions

Target: 5–8 shortlisted. 3 confirmed.

You need 3 for majority-vote scoring.
2 is not enough.

---

**Tweet 6 (the approach)**

First message (don't call it a Turing test):

> "Working on a personal project. Want to run a small experiment — you judge whether two conversations are me or an AI. ~45 min, 2-week window. Interested?"

What NOT to say:
- "Turing test" (wrong frame)
- What you're testing for (anchors evaluation)
- Full protocol upfront (cognitive overload → decline)

---

**Tweet 7 (baseline calibration)**

Before the formal session: have one casual conversation with the evaluator.

Not about the test. Just a normal topic.

This does 3 things:
1. Refreshes their baseline sense of your voice
2. Confirms their memory of you is current
3. Creates a control sample for them to compare against

Most people skip this. Don't.

---

**Tweet 8 (the pipeline)**

Track each candidate through states:

SHORTLISTED → APPROACHED → CONFIRMED → BASELINE_DONE → READY

Don't schedule the formal evaluation until 3 candidates are at READY.

If someone falls out after confirmation: go to backup immediately. Don't wait.

---

**Tweet 9 (what happens when this completes)**

When 3 candidates reach READY:

Trigger G0 of the Turing Test Protocol.

The validation hierarchy has 6 tiers. Deterministic tests are Tier 1. Cross-instance is Tier 5.

Turing test by close friends is Tier 6.

You cannot machine-test your way to Tier 6.

---

**Tweet 10 (close)**

The protocol isn't hard.

The work is finding the right people, approaching them correctly, and running the baseline before the test.

SOP #98 is the bridge between:

Most digital twin projects never cross it.
