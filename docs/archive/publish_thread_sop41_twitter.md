## Tweet 1 — Hook

Your Twitter audience isn't yours. Neither is your YouTube. Neither is your newsletter on Substack. You're a tenant. Here's the 6-gate Platform Persistence Protocol to own your reach.

---

## Tweet 2

G0: Platform Dependency Audit

For every channel: Owned vs. Rented. Can you export the full contact list without asking the platform?

Kill condition: any single rented platform >70% of total reach = critical failure.

No single platform should own your distribution.

---

## Tweet 3

G1: Build your owned channel before scaling any rented one.

Owned hierarchy:
1. Self-hosted email + CRM
2. Email list (Mailchimp/ConvertKit)
3. Self-hosted blog + domain

Rule: never scale Twitter/YouTube/Substack until you can reach people without them.

---

## Tweet 4

G2: Every rented post must funnel to owned.

Thread → bio link → email capture
Video → "free template" → email
Newsletter → "direct list" → owned

Target: ≥5% conversion (rented engagement → owned subscribers).

200 engagements, 0 email subs = a performance, not an asset.

---

## Tweet 5

G3: Content Portability Check — monthly.

All source content in markdown (.md) before publishing.
Monthly: export archive from every rented platform.

Kill condition: if a platform blocks your export → deprioritize it immediately.

Content hostage = data trap.

---

## Tweet 6

G4: Track algorithm dependency monthly.

What % of reach comes from:
→ Algorithm (feed, recommendations)
→ Search (Google, YouTube)
→ Direct (email, typed URL)
→ Referral (links from others)

Target: ≤60% algorithm.

If >80%: you're a supplier for the platform.

---

## Tweet 7

G5: Run the Platform Exit Drill — once a year.

"If my largest rented platform disappeared tomorrow, what % of my audience survives?"

Target: ≥30% portable to owned channel.
<10% = critical gap.

Pre-commit: backup schedule, migration message, owned channel activation path.

---

## Tweet 8

Shadowban: Twitter reach drops 90% overnight.

G0: Twitter = 85% of reach → FAIL
G1: 2,400 email subs → PASS
G2: 3.2% conversion → MARGINAL
G3: All threads in .md → PASS
G4: 89% algorithm → FAIL
G5: 18% portable → FAIL

Email list survived. G1 is the minimum viable layer.

---

## Tweet 9 — CTA

Every platform changes its algorithm, bans accounts, or shuts down eventually.

Distribution without portability is tenancy.

G0 Audit → G1 Own → G2 Seed → G3 Backup → G4 Diversify → G5 Exit Drill
