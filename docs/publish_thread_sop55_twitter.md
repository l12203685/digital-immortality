# Twitter Thread — SOP #55: Environment & Physical Space Protocol

> Slot: Jul 26, 2026
> Domain: 9 (環境設計)
> Hook: "Your environment is making decisions for you."

---

**Tweet 1 (Hook)**
Your environment is making decisions for you.

Every notification. Every object on your desk. Every friction point in your workflow.

These run before you think.

Most people optimize their thinking. Almost no one audits their environment.

Thread: the 5-gate Environment Protocol 🧵

---

**Tweet 2**
First: what "environment making decisions" actually means.

Your brain has finite decision bandwidth (SOP #53).

Every unplanned context switch, every visual distraction, every 3-click tool access costs bandwidth before you even start working.

You're not lazy. Your environment is poorly configured.

---

**Tweet 3**
G0: Weekly Environment Audit (5 min)

Three signals:
• Visual noise — count irrelevant objects/apps in default view
• Friction score — steps between you and your most-used tool (target: ≤1)
• Interrupt rate — unplanned context switches per cognitive peak hour

If interrupt rate > 3/hour during peak: you don't have a focus problem. You have a structural problem.

---

**Tweet 4**
G1: Derivative Scan

Don't ask "is my environment good today?"

Ask: is friction trending up or down this week?

Three derivatives:
• ΔFriction/week
• ΔInterrupt rate/week
• ΔDefault gravity (are your defaults pulling toward priority work, or away?)

Single bad day = noise. Three consecutive = structural signal.

---

**Tweet 5**
G2: Non-Negotiable Environment Budget

Four recurring costs (not one-time fixes):

• 2-min workspace reset end of day (prevents accumulation)
• Monthly notification audit (defaults are always pro-interrupt)
• Quarterly default alignment (browser, phone, desktop — do they serve current priority?)
• Monthly friction scan (top 3 tools still ≤1 step?)

These are infrastructure costs. Skip them and environment decays silently.

---

**Tweet 6**
The compounding problem:

Environment degradation is invisible until it's severe.

You don't notice the 4th notification source you added.
You don't notice the desk getting 3 objects heavier.
You don't notice the tool that added 2 more clicks.

But your interrupt rate went from 2/hour to 6/hour.

That's 4 context switches per hour stolen from L3 work.

---

**Tweet 7**
G3: Quarterly Leverage Scan

Which single environment change would most reduce cognitive overhead?

Run once per quarter. Environment leverage changes slowly.

Protocol:
1. List all friction points
2. Identify highest-interrupt source
3. Implement ONE change (not a stack)
4. Measure for 2 weeks
5. If interrupt rate ↓1/hour or friction ↓20% → write to default

One isolated change. Attributable result. Keep or discard.

---

**Tweet 8**
The anti-pattern: "productivity overhaul"

10 changes simultaneously.
Productivity improves.
You don't know which change caused it.
3 weeks later, you've reverted most of them.

Isolate. Measure. Keep. This is the only way to build a compound environment.

---

**Tweet 9**
G4: Weekly Review (10 min, Sunday)

Three questions:
1. Did environment support or undermine priority work? (Y/N)
2. If N → behavioral / structural / external interrupt source?
3. Persistent pattern (2+ weeks)? → change a default, not willpower

Classification matters. "I got distracted" is not a cause. "My phone was on my desk with notifications on" is.

---

**Tweet 10**
G5: Environment Emergency

Trigger: interrupt rate > 5/hour during cognitive peak × 3 consecutive days.

Protocol:
1. 15-min full reset (non-negotiable before any work)
2. Audit all interrupt sources from last 3 days
3. Kill non-essentials (disable, not mute)
4. Restore top tools to ≤1-step access
5. No L3 decisions until G0 returns to baseline × 2 days

Environment repair IS the highest-derivative work during emergency. Not catching up.

---

**Tweet 11**
Three-layer structure:

L1 (Execute): G2 maintenance runs automatically. Daily reset, monthly notification audit.

L2 (Evaluate): G0/G1 audit catches drift before it compounds. G4 classifies cause.

L3 (Evolve): G3 quarterly scan restructures defaults. G4 triggers protocol edits when pattern confirmed.

L1 without L2+L3 = slow decay. Reviews detect it. Quarterly scans restructure it.
