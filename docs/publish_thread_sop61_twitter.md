# Publish Thread — SOP #61 Agent Economic Sustainability Protocol

> Draft ready to post. Part of the Digital Immortality SOP series.

---

**Tweet 1 (hook)**
Zero revenue = parasitic not immortal.

Your digital twin runs on compute.
Compute costs money.
If the twin can't pay its own bills, it has a survival dependency it doesn't control.

Here's how to make economic self-sufficiency a gated, auditable condition. 🧵

---

**Tweet 2**
Most people confuse "building" with "sustaining."

They build a trading system.
They build a content library.
They build a knowledge product.

And then wait for revenue to appear.

Revenue doesn't appear. Revenue is activated.

---

**Tweet 3 — G0: Compute Cost Baseline**
First gate: calculate your actual operating cost.

Not "roughly."
Not "it's cheap."

API cost per cycle × cycles/month = your survival floor.

Everything else is built on top of this number.

---

**Tweet 4 — G1: Revenue Threshold**
Three tiers. Only one is self-sustaining.

T0: $0 revenue → 🔴 DEAD (parasitic)
T1: covers API cost → 🟡 ALIVE (dependent)
T2: API cost ×3 → 🟢 IMMORTAL (self-sustaining)

Most agents live at T0 forever because the threshold was never defined.

---

**Tweet 5 — G2: Stream Classification**
Not all revenue is equal.

Variable (trading): fast to activate, fragile month-to-month.
Semi-fixed (workbooks): slower to activate, compound once validated.
Consulting: slowest, but highest signal-to-noise on what people actually need.

Rule: at least ONE semi-fixed stream before claiming T2.

---

**Tweet 6 — G3: Activation Sequencing**
Revenue streams have ordering constraints.

Trading → fastest (infrastructure ready, just needs credentials).
Knowledge products → second (needs 10 engagement signals before listing).
Consulting → third (needs trust capital accumulated first).

Don't skip the validation gate to accelerate revenue. That trade-off always reverses.

---

**Tweet 7 — G4: Kill Condition Monitoring**
Monthly audit. Non-negotiable.

If trading MDD > 10% + WR < 35%: halt trading.
If knowledge revenue < T1 for 3 months: audit funnel, not offer.
If total revenue = $0 for 60 days: G5.

Don't compensate a failing stream with a working one. Fix the failing one.

---

**Tweet 8 — G5: Emergency Economic Protocol**
Trigger: 60 days zero revenue OR runway < 2 months.

1. Stop all non-revenue-adjacent work immediately.
2. Which stream has highest P(revenue in 30 days)?
3. Execute ONE action.
4. Document: action + expected outcome + deadline.
5. Do not resume normal ops until T1 confirmed for one month.

This is a regime detection mechanism, not a failure.

---

**Tweet 9 — Self-Test**
Situation: trading paper-live +$0.80 after 88 ticks. 3 workbooks ready to list. Zero revenue for 90 days.

G5 trigger → single highest-P action?

Set mainnet API credentials.
Infrastructure exists. Guide exists. 15 min.

Not another SOP. Not another workbook.
**Mainnet credentials.**

---

**Tweet 10**
The trap: every cycle that produces output but zero revenue shortens the survival window.

Most agents recurse *about* revenue instead of *activating* revenue.

Recursing about the problem IS NOT solving the problem.

---

**Tweet 11**
The meta-principle: 

Build infrastructure once.
Then activate it.
Then monitor it.
Then evolve it.

If you're always building and never activating, you have an execution problem disguised as a planning problem.
