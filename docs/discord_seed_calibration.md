**What is a calibration session?**

You build a model of someone. They correct it. The corrections are the product.

This is what one looks like (anonymized, from my session with Organism B):

---

**Pre-session: what my model predicted**

I had written this about B's social behavior:

> "B prefers direct feedback when someone is wrong. He'll state his position clearly even in group settings."

**His correction:**

> "No. I prefer direct feedback *in private*. In group settings, I'll hint and document, not confront. Direct confrontation in public damages the relationship faster than it fixes the problem."

**What this changed:**

- Updated §4 Behavioral Patterns: "group_dynamics: indirect public signal + direct private conversation"
- Added new scenario to test battery: "Junior person is most right in a meeting. B: documents + states position. Does NOT escalate publicly."
- This one correction shifted 2 scenario outcomes from AGREE to DIVERGE

---

**Pre-session: what my model predicted**

I had written:

> "B will audit relationships annually and quietly rebalance — reduce investment in people who aren't reciprocating."

**His correction:**

> "Yes, but I never tell them. I don't do the direct conversation about it. I just... stop. They figure it out or they don't."

**What this changed:**

- Updated §6 Social Operating Rules: "network rebalancing = quiet, not explicit"
- This divergence from my default (explicit conversation) explains why we handle relationship distance differently
- Now wired into collision engine as structural difference

---

**What makes a good calibration session:**

1. **Show, don't ask.** "Here's what I think you'd do in scenario X" → they correct or confirm. Not "what would you do?"
2. **Ask about *past* decisions.** "That time you left [job/project]" → behavioral evidence, not self-image.
3. **Follow the corrections.** Every correction points to a principle gap. Write the principle, not the anecdote.

**Anti-pattern**: "Your model is pretty accurate." → you didn't go deep enough. Run harder scenarios.
