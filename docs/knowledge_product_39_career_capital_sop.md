# SOP #39 — Career Capital Accumulation Protocol

**Domain:** 8 — 生活維護 (Life Maintenance / Career Infrastructure)
**Date:** 2026-04-09 UTC
**DNA Anchors:** MD-28, MD-27, MD-210, MD-271, MD-231, MD-204

---

## Introduction

Most people manage their career the way amateur traders manage a position: reactively, without pre-defined thresholds, and benchmarked against whatever they last saw rather than what the market actually pays. Career capital — the compounded value of your skills, brand, relationships, and track record — is the only asset that follows you regardless of employer. It compounds when maintained intentionally and decays under neglect. This SOP enforces six structural gates for auditing, growing, and deploying career capital so that every job transition, offer negotiation, or role decision operates from real data — not gut feel or peer anchoring.

---

## Gate G0 — Career Capital Audit

**Trigger:** Once at initialization; revisit every 12 months or before any job-transition decision.

**Action:** Map your current career capital across four dimensions.

| Dimension | Definition | Key signal |
|-----------|-----------|-----------|
| **Skills** | What you can do that others cannot, or do faster/better | Can you teach this? Can it move to a new employer tomorrow? |
| **Brand** | What you are publicly known for (search results, referrals, reputation) | What does your name surface when Googled or mentioned in your field? |
| **Track record** | Concrete, quantified outcomes from prior roles | Numbers: revenue driven, teams led, systems built, metrics moved |
| **Network** | Who in your field you can access within 1 hop | Quality over quantity — can you reach decision-makers? |

**Action:**
1. For each dimension, write 3 specific, verifiable examples — not adjectives. ("I built the pricing model that reduced margin leakage by 14%" not "I'm detail-oriented.")
2. Rate each dimension from 1–5. 1 = visible weakness, 5 = recognized domain authority.
3. Identify the lowest-rated dimension. That is the bottleneck.

**Rule:** Career capital scales as the product of all four dimensions, not the sum. A 5 in skills with a 1 in brand compounds slowly. A 4×4×4×4 outperforms a 5×5×5×1.

**Pass criteria:** A written inventory exists with 3 specific examples per dimension. A bottleneck has been identified.

**Kill condition:** If no dimension scores above 2 → you are pre-compounding. All effort should go to skills first. No negotiation, no transition until at least one dimension reaches 4.

## Gate G1 — Salary Floor Calculation

**Trigger:** Before any offer comparison, salary discussion, or renewal negotiation.

**Action:** Calculate your exact salary floor from first principles — not from your last salary.

**Formula (MD-28):**

```
Current_comp = base + bonus (average 3yr) + equity (vested annual value) + benefits
Floor = Current_comp × (1 + minimum_acceptable_growth_rate)
```

**Default growth rate minimum:** 15% total comp increase for any external move. Adjust for:
- Market conditions (contraction: 10% minimum; expansion: 20%+)
- Role change (lateral: 15%; step-up: 25%)
- Geography change: recalculate PPP-adjusted

**Rule (MD-28):** Never enter an offer comparison without a floor calculated in advance. "How does this feel?" is not a calculation. Anchor to the floor, not to the offer you just received.

**Action:**
1. Write the exact number in your local currency. Not a range.
2. Write the breakdown: what components make up your current comp?
3. Write the floor: Floor = Current_total × 1.15 (or calibrated rate).

**Pass criteria:** A single, written number. Not "around 120k." Exactly 120,000.

**Kill condition:** If you cannot calculate your current total comp within 15 minutes → you do not know what you are worth. Before any negotiation, close this information gap first.

## Gate G2 — Market Rate Verification

**Trigger:** Before any offer comparison. Also annually, independent of transition plans.

**Action:** Confirm the salary ceiling for your role — not from public job listings but from internal industry sources.

**Rule (MD-271):** Public salary ranges are averages and lag the market by 12–18 months. The real ceiling is visible only through internal information: peers' compensation, recruiter off-the-record conversations, and closed communities.

**Information hierarchy (most reliable to least):**
1. Direct conversation with peer at competing firm (within 6 months)
2. Trusted recruiter's off-the-record ceiling for your profile
3. Industry-specific salary database (not LinkedIn Salary, not Glassdoor)
4. Public job listings at target comp (lagged, use as floor not ceiling)

**Action:**
1. Name 3 people in your industry who are 1–2 years ahead and have comparable scope. What are they making?
2. Speak to 1 recruiter per quarter, even if not actively seeking. Track what they quote.
3. Record the ceiling: the highest credible total comp you could reach with your current profile.

**Pass criteria:** A ceiling number with a source and date. "I believe" is not a source.

**Kill condition:** If market ceiling < floor from G1 → the role type, not the employer, is the bottleneck. Transitioning within the same role category will not move your ceiling. Decision: invest in a new skill dimension (back to G0) or accept the ceiling.

## Gate G3 — Offer Comparison Framework

**Trigger:** When 2+ concrete offers exist (or 1 offer vs. current role "stay offer").

**Action:** Build a decision tree. Do not compare intuitively.

**Framework (MD-210):**

```
For each offer:
  Total comp score = base + bonus (expected, not max) + equity (current price × vesting) + benefits
  Opportunity cost = (Floor from G1) - (Total comp score)
  Upside horizon: "In 3 years, what is the likely comp?"
```

**Comparison table:**

| | Current Role | Offer A | Offer B |
|--|--|--|--|
| Total comp (year 1) | | | |
| Total comp (year 3 estimated) | | | |
| Role leverage (does this compound?) | | | |
| Optionality (doors this opens) | | | |
| Kill conditions (when would I exit?) | | | |

**Rule:** Never accept the best year-1 offer if it has a worse 3-year trajectory. Comp is a trajectory, not a snapshot.

**Pass criteria:** The table is filled in with numbers, not adjectives. A single recommended option is named with the reason it wins on the 3-year trajectory.

**Kill condition:** If two options are within 5% on both year-1 and year-3 comp → comp is not the deciding variable. Move to optionality and role leverage as tiebreaker.

## Gate G4 — Negotiation Sequencing

**Trigger:** When an offer is received and you are asked for compensation expectations.

**Action:** Never anchor first (MD-27).

**Rule (MD-27):** When asked for a salary number, buy time before responding. "Allow me a few days to evaluate the full package." The first number in a negotiation becomes the anchor. Your anchor should come from G1 (Floor) and G2 (Ceiling), not from their opening number or your discomfort.

**Sequence:**
1. Receive offer → do NOT give a counter-number immediately
2. Run G1 + G2 fully if not already complete
3. Counter with the G2 ceiling number as total comp target
4. Accept pushback only with written justification. "Budget" without a number is not a justification.

**Phrase library:**
- "I'd like a few days to review the full package before giving you a number." ← never give real-time
- "Based on my research and scope, I'm targeting [ceiling]. Is there flexibility?" ← anchor to your number
- "I'm comfortable at [floor] if the equity acceleration is structured as [X]." ← trade comp for structure

**Pass criteria:** No number given until G1/G2 are complete. Counter anchored to ceiling from G2.

**Kill condition:** If you have already given a number below G1 floor → introduce a new variable (sign-on, equity refresh, scope increase) that lifts total comp without "changing" the base you anchored.

## Gate G5 — Career Brand Maintenance

**Trigger:** Monthly (5-minute check). Revisit G0 audit annually.

**Action:** Your career brand is defined by the most recent high-signal moment, not your full history (MD-204).

**Rule (MD-204):** Recruiters and hiring managers remember the most recent 2–3 things associated with your name. Not the award from 4 years ago. What happened recently?

**Signal maintenance actions (in order of leverage):**
1. **Visible output** — write, speak, post, or ship something demonstrating your top dimension at least once per 90 days
2. **Highlight refresh** — update your profile headline/summary at least once per 6 months
3. **Inbound audit** — have you been contacted for a reason that reflects your target brand in the last 90 days? If no → brand is invisible or misaligned
4. **Reference readiness** — 2 people who can say something specific and concrete about your last contribution

**Kill conditions:**
- No visible output in >90 days → produce one this week
- No inbound from target direction in >180 days → diagnose: skill / brand / network gap
- Last concrete highlight >18 months old → execute new high-signal project or reframe existing one

**Pass criteria:** Recent output within 90 days. Profile reflects current scope. ≥1 inbound from target direction in past 6 months.

## Self-Test

Scenario: You receive a recruiter message offering 20% base bump. Performance review in 6 weeks.

**G0:** Does the new role compound your weakest dimension, or replicate existing work?

**G1:** Calculate exact current comp including this year's bonus and equity vest. 20% bump on base is 20% of what?

**G2:** Internal sources suggest the ceiling for your profile is 35%. Their opening is suppressed by 15%.

**G3:** Year-1 is 20% higher. Year-3 trajectory: flat ceiling at both companies. Premium is one-time, not structural.

**G4:** Tell the recruiter you need 2 weeks. Do not give a number until G1/G2 are complete. Counter with 35% (G2 ceiling).

**G5:** Would this role produce a brandable output within 12 months? Or replicate your existing portfolio?

Recommended action: Request full written offer. Counter from G2 ceiling. Accept only if year-3 trajectory and role leverage justify transition cost.

## Connection to Financial Freedom Framework

Career capital is the primary input for accelerating the Tier 0 → Tier 1 transition (SOP #21). Every percentage point of salary floor increase compounds: a 15% raise at age 30 vs. 35 is not a 15% difference — it is a 5-year compounding difference in the capital base deployed toward Tier 0 funding. Negotiate correctly → deploy surplus → compound toward Tier 1 faster.
