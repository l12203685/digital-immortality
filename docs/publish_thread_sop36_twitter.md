# Twitter Thread — SOP #36: Barbell Life Strategy: Asymmetric Optionality Protocol

---

**1/**
Most people's life strategy has no barbell.

They take existential risk on their main income and cap their upside on their experiments.

Exactly backwards.

Here's the correct structure — and the 6-gate protocol to build it. 🧵

---

**2/**
The barbell failure mode looks like this:

→ Concentrated job risk (one employer, one skill, one market)
→ Side projects funded from savings, sized to "not lose too much"

You've capped the one thing that should have unlimited upside.
And left the one thing that should be safe fully exposed.

---

**3/**
The correct structure:

STABLE (large): income floor, cash runway, primary skills
Must survive any bad scenario. Zero leverage. No existential risk.

EXPLOSIVE (small): experiments, new skills, side bets
Accept 100% loss. No cap on upside. Sized so full loss ≠ catastrophe.

Never mix the pools.

---

**4/**
G0 — Classify before you evaluate.

Every decision is either STABLE or EXPLOSIVE. Label it first.

STABLE: income, housing, core health, primary relationships
EXPLOSIVE: side projects, new skills, career experiments, speculative bets

If you can't classify it cleanly: treat it as EXPLOSIVE.

---

**5/**
G1 — Cap the downside, or don't enter.

STABLE: if the loss scenario is existential, it fails the barbell. You cannot speculate with it.

EXPLOSIVE: write the full-loss number before entry. If you can't accept it, reduce size.

Rule: preserve principal. Gamble with interest only. Never reverse this.

---

**6/**
G2 — 10-year survival check.

Ask: will this STABLE element still exist in 10 years?

Industry disrupted? Platform dead? Skill commoditized?

If uncertain → it's not STABLE. It's a slow-moving EXPLOSIVE bet wearing STABLE clothing.

Reclassify. Build a real floor.

---

**7/**
G3 — EV independence test.

Each bet must have positive EV on its own — regardless of whether other bets succeed.

"If X works, then Y will work" = correlation trap.
Two bets that can only win together are one bet with double exposure.

Break them into sequential stages. Never combine correlated bets.

---

**8/**
G4 — Optionality has a price.

Decisions that expand options: no premium required.
Decisions that contract options: require explicit compensation.

If the expected gain doesn't exceed the value of the path you're closing — preserve optionality.

Default: keep the door open unless you're paid to close it.

---

**9/**
G5 — Write exits before entry.

Three conditions, written before you deploy capital:
1. Time limit: "If X months pass without Y, I exit."
2. Capital limit: "If I lose Z, I exit."
3. Signal threshold: "If I see W, the thesis is dead."

Cannot write them? Do not enter. No thesis = no bet.

---

**10/**
Self-test: should you leave your job to trade full-time?

G0: Job = STABLE. Trading = EXPLOSIVE. Cannot run both at full leverage.
G1: No income + trading = existential risk. Fails barbell. Need 24-month cash runway first.
G2: Will your trading edge survive 10 years? Uncertain → trading is EXPLOSIVE, not a salary replacement.

---

**11/**
G4: Leaving employment contracts major optionality.

Required compensation: trading income must exceed job income × 1.5–2×.
If it doesn't clear that threshold: stay employed, trade part-time, build the floor.

G5 pre-write: "If I haven't replaced 80% of salary in 12 months, I return to employment."

Entry permitted only after all gates pass.

---

**12/**
The kill conditions:

→ STABLE drops below 18-month coverage: exit all EXPLOSIVE immediately
→ EXPLOSIVE EV goes negative after 3 data points: exit, don't average down
→ Can't write exit conditions: don't enter

The barbell only works if the STABLE stays stable.
Protect the floor. Everything else is noise.
