# Twitter Thread — SOP #45: Growing Your Decision Network from 2 to N

**Domain:** 4 — 社交圈 / organism scale
**Date:** 2026-04-09 UTC (Cycle 206)
**Hook:** "Two organisms produce 1 collision. Five produce 10. The network compounds — but only after you get past the 2nd."

---

**1/**
Two organisms produce 1 collision.
Five produce 10.
Ten produce 45.

The network compounds.

But almost everyone stalls at 2.

Here's why — and the fix.

🧵

---

**2/**
The stall pattern:

→ You build a model of Friend B
→ The calibration session never happens (scheduling)
→ You don't invite Friend C until B is "done"

Serialized. B's delay blocks everything.

The fix: recruitment and calibration run in parallel.

---

**3/**
Before you invite anyone, you need one demo.

One collision report you can show in 90 seconds.

Not a concept. A real output: "Here are the 7 domains where Samuel and I think completely differently."

If you can't demo in 90 seconds, you can't recruit.

---

**4/**
The recruitment pitch is NOT "I built an AI model of you."

It's:

"I built a model of how Samuel makes decisions. Before we talked, I ran it against mine. 7 domains where we diverge — all social. I showed him the list. He spent 20 min correcting the wrong parts. Now I have a precise map of where my model of him was broken.

I want to do the same with you."

Lead with the output. The output is a map of your blind spots about them.

---

**5/**
When in-person is blocked: async calibration.

3 steps over 3 days:

1. Send §0 Core Principles as text: "Mark what's wrong."
2. Send 3 scenarios from divergence domains. Wait for their answers.
3. Follow up on 1–2 surprising ones: "What principle drove that?"

Their exact language → new principle in the DNA.

Minimum for validation: 3 corrected + 1 new principle they added themselves.

---

**6/**
Organism C selection criteria:

✓ They've surprised you 3+ times in the same domain
✓ They'd correct wrong inferences (not flatter you)
✓ 1+ year of observed behavior
✓ Known divergence from Organism B in at least one domain

If they always agree with you → mirror, not organism.

The collision produces signal where your model has gaps. Build where the gaps are.

---

**7/**
The triangle is the richest signal format.

3 organisms → 3 pair collisions.

But the real output is this:

A and B diverge on domain X.
C agrees with A.

Now you know X is a *value split*, not a style difference.

You can't distinguish the two with only 2 organisms. You need the third.

---

**8/**
Discord is the network layer.

But an empty Discord is an orphan. No one joins a ghost server.

Seed content first:

• `#collision-reports` → your real collision report (one real output)
• `#divergence-of-the-week` → one divergence framed as a question
• `#organism-drafts` → your own §0 — invite others to mark what they'd answer differently

One real post per channel. Then invite.

---

**9/**
The acquisition loop:

1. Build Organism C (someone in your network)
2. Show C the collision report
3. C asks "what's the Discord?" → ready answer
4. C invites 1 person from their network

At 5 organisms, the network is self-sustaining.

Each collision produces content that attracts the next organism.

---

**10/**
Scale ceiling: 7–10 active organisms per person.

Beyond that: maintenance > insight. Diminishing returns.

The goal isn't maximum organisms.

It's maximum precision. A small network of well-calibrated organisms beats a large one of hollow models.
