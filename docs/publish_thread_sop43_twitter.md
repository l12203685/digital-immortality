# Twitter Thread — SOP #43: Second-Order Relationship Effects

**Domain:** 4 — 社交資本
**Date:** 2026-04-09T UTC (Cycle 204)
**Queue position:** Week 20+ (post-Apr 9 launch)

---

**1/**
Most people think relationships are discrete: you know Person A, you know Person B.

That's wrong. Relationships are a *network* — and networks compound.

Here's the Second-Order Relationship Protocol that most people never discover.

🧵

---

**2/**
The math most people miss:

You have 5 close relationships.
Each knows 50 people you don't.
If they each introduce you to 3/year = 15 introductions.
Those 15 each know 50 more people...

You're not managing 5 relationships. You're managing access to 750+ potential nodes.

---

**3/**
G0 — Network Position Audit (run quarterly)

For each Tier 1/2 node, assess:
→ How many people do they know that you don't?
→ Are those people in domains you want to enter?
→ Have they actively introduced you to anyone in the past 12 months?

Gate pass: ≥3 nodes introduced you to someone in the past 12 months.

---

**4/**
If you fail G0 — your network is a static asset, not a compounding one.

You have the connections. You're just not activating their compounding property.

The difference between a 5-person static network and a 5-person compounding network is everything.

---

**5/**
G1 — Compounding Activation

Before you can request introductions, you need:
① Behavior verification (3 months — they introduce, not just talk about introducing)
② Shared output (co-wrote, solved something together, credited them publicly)
③ You made 2-3 introductions for them first

You can't extract compounding you haven't invested.

---

**6/**
G2 — The correct introduction ask

Wrong: "Can you introduce me to anyone in VC?"

Right:
"You mentioned [specific person] works in exactly what I'm researching. Would a 30-min intro call be appropriate? Here's 3 sentences you could forward: [prepared context]"

Specificity + preparation = yes.

---

**7/**
Three things that make an introduction request work:
① Specific person, specific context (not fishing)
② Pre-written forward text (remove all friction from the introducer)
③ "Would it be appropriate?" — respects their judgment of the relationship

Make it a copy-paste. They say yes or no in 10 seconds.

---

**8/**
G3 — Second-Order Activation

Every successful introduction creates a new node.

Immediately audit: does the new connection have high network density? Are they an active introducer?

If yes: add to G1 queue. You're now one introduction deeper into the network.

This is where compounding starts.

---

**9/**
G4 — Anti-Decay Maintenance

Networks decay to your investment rate. If you invest nothing, connections go cold in 6-12 months.

Tier 1 (≤5): weekly, 1 shared output/quarter
Tier 2 (≤20): monthly, 1 substantive exchange/quarter
Tier 3 (≤100): quarterly signal

"Happy birthday" doesn't reset the clock.

---

**10/**
The key insight Samuel's DNA has that most people miss:

"Relationships are the alpha — network density compounds faster than any investment."

Not because relationships are nice. Because second-order effects are exponential, and most people only invest in first-order connections.
