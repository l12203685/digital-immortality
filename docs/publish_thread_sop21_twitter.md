# Publish Thread — SOP #21: Financial Freedom Tier Framework

**Hook tweet:**

> Most people don't have a financial freedom plan.
> They have a financial freedom fantasy.
> 
> The difference: one has thresholds, tiers, and rule sets.
> The other has "someday."
> 
> Here's how to turn the fantasy into a decision system. 🧵

---

**Tweet 2:**

> Financial freedom is not one destination. It's three regime changes.
> 
> Tier 0: Your passive income covers survival.
> Tier 1: Your passive income covers your actual life.
> Tier 2: Your net worth doubles every decade automatically.
> 
> Each tier has completely different rules. Most people apply Tier 2 rules while stuck in Tier 0.

---

**Tweet 3:**

> Tier 0 — Defensive Base (存活線)
> 
> Threshold: monthly_costs × 12 / 0.035
> 
> If your monthly costs are $3,500 → Tier 0 = $1.2M
> 
> Until you're here: NO leverage on base capital. All surplus → Tier 1 build.
> Protection mode, not growth mode.

---

**Tweet 4:**

> Tier 1 — Operational Freedom (自由線)
> 
> Threshold: annual_lifestyle_spend / 0.035
> 
> At this tier: employment becomes optional, not mandatory.
> 
> The decision: "Would I do this job for free? If yes → stay. If no → renegotiate or exit."
> 
> Not before. Not based on vibes.

---

**Tweet 5:**

> Tier 2 — Anti-Fragile Compound (複利線)
> 
> Threshold: Tier 1 capital × 3
> 
> The 3x buffer absorbs a full market crash without requiring you to liquidate.
> 
> Rule: lifestyle stays locked at Tier 1 level. All surplus compounds.
> 
> You don't inflate. You compound.

---

**Tweet 6:**

> The 5-gate decision protocol before any major capital move:
> 
> G0: Which tier am I in right now? (Calculate. Don't guess.)
> G1: Apply THAT tier's rule set. Not the one above.
> G2: Leverage checklist — worst case in dollars, <10% of tier threshold, friction pre-modeled.
> G3: Is my δ (capital/threshold) positive?
> G4: Is my barbell intact? (80% stable / 20% explosive)

---

**Tweet 7:**

> The barbell rule (G4) is the load-bearing structure.
> 
> 80%+ → durable assets (survives 50% equity crash without forced liquidation)
> ≤20% → explosive bucket (trading capital, concentrated bets)
> 
> If ratio drifts outside 70/30 → rebalance before next deployment.
> 
> This is not a preference. It's a structural constraint.

---

**Tweet 8:**

> Real-world self-test:
> 
> Net worth: $320k
> Monthly expenses: $3,500
> Trading strategy: +$800/month avg
> Considering: quitting job
> 
> Tier 1 threshold = $3,500 × 12 / 0.035 = $1,200,000
> 
> You're at 27% funded.
> Time to Tier 1 at $800/mo: ~91 years.
> 
> The decision is now obvious.

---

**Tweet 9:**

> The most common failure modes:
> 
> • Tier inflation — "I'm almost there" → spending before funded
> • Leverage before Tier 0 — margin loans while base is unfunded
> • Conflating tiers — "$500k feels like freedom" (Tier 1 might need $1.2M)
> • Ignoring δ — capital growing but too slowly to reach next tier
> • Emotional withdrawal — pulling growth assets when market drops 30%
> 
> Name the failure mode. It stops working on you.

---

**Tweet 10:**

> This system connects to the full architecture:
> 
> SOP #01: your trading strategy is the Tier 1→Tier 2 engine
> SOP #08: the barbell maps to three-bucket capital isolation
> SOP #14: Tier 1 funding is the full-time switch threshold
> SOP #18: Tier 0 funded = inaction bias fully activated
> 
> Each SOP is a node. This is the frame they hang on.

---

**Tweet 11:**

> The most important insight:
> 
> You can't apply Tier 2 thinking in Tier 0.
> 
> When you're unfunded, the job is not optional. Leverage is not a growth tool. 
> Lifestyle inflation is a compounding error.
> 
> Regime determines rules. Rules determine decisions.
> Get the regime right first.
