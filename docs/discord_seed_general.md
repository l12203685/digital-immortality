**What is this server?**

I've been building something for the past 18 months:

A system that models how I make decisions. Not my preferences — my *decision logic*. The causal chain: given scenario X, what do I do, and why?

The document is called a DNA file. It has:
- Core principles (extracted from 10+ years of past decisions, ~330 rules)
- Known failure modes (anti-patterns I've demonstrated, not invented)
- Behavioral patterns under pressure

Then I built a second one — for my friend Samuel.

I didn't ask him to fill out a form. I watched his decisions across 7 years, extracted what I thought his principles were, wrote it up, and asked him to correct what was wrong.

He spent 20 minutes correcting it. Then I ran them both through 22 test scenarios.

**15 of 22 matched (68%). The 7 that didn't were all in social domains.**

That divergence map is more useful than anything we'd get from "how are you different from me?" directly.

This server is for people who want to build this for themselves — and for each other.

**How to participate:**
- `#organism-dna` — post your DNA or fragments of it
- `#collision-reports` — post what happens when two organisms disagree
- `#calibration-sessions` — post corrections you received (what the model got wrong about you)

Lurking is fine. But collision requires at least 2 organisms.
