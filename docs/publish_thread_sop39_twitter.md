## Tweet 1 — Hook

Most people benchmark their salary against their last job.

That's the worst anchor possible.

Your last employer anchored it. Their budget anchored it. Your discomfort anchored it.

None of that is the market.

Here's the 6-gate Career Capital Protocol. 12 tweets.

---

## Tweet 2

Career capital is the compounded value of:
- What you can do (skills)
- What you're known for (brand)
- What you've proven (track record)
- Who you can access (network)

Most people only think about the paycheck.

The paycheck is the output.
Career capital is the input.

---

## Tweet 3

**Gate 0: Career Capital Audit**

Score yourself 1–5 on four dimensions:
- Skills (teachable, portable?)
- Brand (what surfaces when you're mentioned?)
- Track record (quantified outcomes, not adjectives)
- Network (can you reach decision-makers in 1 hop?)

Career capital scales as a PRODUCT, not a sum.

5×5×5×1 = stuck.

---

## Tweet 4

The most common trap:

Scoring 5 in skills.
Scoring 1 in brand.

Nobody hires what they don't know about.

Your skills compound inside your head.
Your brand compounds outside it.

Both matter. Neither replaces the other.

---

## Tweet 5

**Gate 1: Salary Floor Calculation**

Not from your last salary.
From first principles:

Floor = (base + avg bonus + vested equity + benefits) × 1.15

That 1.15 is not random.
It's the minimum premium for bearing the cost of switching:
– Time lost
– Risk taken
– Optionality surrendered

If the offer doesn't clear the floor, you're subsidizing their hiring.

---

## Tweet 6

**Gate 2: Market Rate Verification**

Public salary ranges are:
- Averages (not ceilings)
- 12–18 months lagged
- Biased toward self-reported data

The real ceiling is in internal information:
- Direct peer conversations
- Off-the-record recruiter quotes
- Closed industry communities

Know the ceiling before you negotiate.
You can't anchor to what you don't know.

---

## Tweet 7

**Gate 3: Offer Comparison Framework**

Don't compare year-1 comp.
Compare year-3 trajectories.

A 20% raise now with a flat ceiling for 3 years is a worse trade than 10% now with a role that doubles your ceiling.

The question is never "which pays more?"
The question is "which compounds faster?"

---

## Tweet 8

**Gate 4: Negotiation Sequencing**

They ask: "What's your salary expectation?"

Wrong answer: any specific number, immediately.

Correct answer: "Allow me a few days to evaluate the full package."

Why:
– The first number anchors the negotiation
– Your anchor should come from G1 + G2, not from their offer
– Buying time is not stalling. It's the correct move.

---

## Tweet 9

Once you're ready to counter:

1. Anchor to your G2 ceiling, not their offer
2. Break down the comp (base vs equity vs sign-on)
3. Accept pushback only with written justification
4. Never re-anchor lower without extracting something new

"Budget" is not a justification.
"What's your budget?" is a real question.

---

## Tweet 10

**Gate 5: Career Brand Maintenance**

Your brand is defined by your most recent high-signal moment.

Not the award from 4 years ago.
Not the project from a prior employer.
What happened in the last 90 days?

Maintenance cadence:
- Visible output: every 90 days minimum
- Profile refresh: every 6 months
- Reference readiness: 2 people who can name a concrete contribution

---

## Tweet 11

Diagnosis tree for brand decay:

No inbound in 180 days?
→ Is your skill visible? → Brand gap
→ Is your skill not competitive? → Skill gap
→ Do people know you exist? → Network gap

Each gap has a different fix.
"Post more" only fixes one of them.

---

## Tweet 12 — Close

The Career Capital Protocol:

G0 → Audit 4 dimensions (skills/brand/track/network as a product)
G1 → Calculate floor from comp math, not gut feel
G2 → Get ceiling from internal sources, not job boards
G3 → Compare 3-year trajectories, not year-1 numbers
G4 → Never give a number first
G5 → Maintain visible output every 90 days

Comp is a snapshot.
Career capital is the trajectory.
